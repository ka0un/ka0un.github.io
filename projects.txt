# Finding Hidden Sales Leads on Autopilot (TSR) 2026 Jan

**A custom automation system that quietly hunts down new companies for a sales team — every single day, without anyone lifting a finger.**

---

## The Client

A Japanese enterprise whose sales team relied on a large internal database of companies to find and qualify new business. To keep that database useful, someone had to constantly track down companies that weren't in it yet — fresh prospects worth reaching out to.

## The Problem

For a long time, this was a manual job. A team member would visit popular job-posting sites by hand, scroll through listings, copy down company names, and then check each one against the internal database to see if it was already known. Anything new went on a list for the sales team.

It worked, but barely. The process leaned entirely on one or two people, so the quality changed depending on who did it and how much time they had that week. Companies slipped through the cracks. Worse, Japanese company names are written in many different ways — full names, abbreviations, slight spelling differences — which made matching them against existing records slow and error-prone. An earlier attempt to automate parts of it kept running into strict time limits on the tools they'd tried, so it never became something they could depend on.

In short: they knew valuable leads were out there, but they had no reliable way to find them. They'd never had a system that simply did this for them.

## What We Built

We built exactly that — a system that does the entire job on its own, start to finish.

Every day, it automatically visits the major job sites where these companies advertise, gathers the company names, and tidies them up so that different spellings of the same business are recognized as one. It then checks each company against the client's internal database. Anything genuinely new gets added straight into their system as a fresh lead, and the sales team gets a short summary so they know what turned up — no digging required.

What used to be hours of tedious, easy-to-skip manual work now happens quietly in the background, on schedule, with consistent quality every time.

## How We Delivered It

We rolled the project out in two clear stages so the client could see and trust the results before everything went live.

**Stage one** focused on the hard part: reliably collecting the company information, cleaning it up, and matching the names correctly. The output was a simple, organized spreadsheet the team could review. This let everyone confirm the system was finding the right companies before we connected it to anything important.

**Stage two** connected that proven pipeline directly into the client's existing tools. New leads now flow automatically into their database, and the sales team receives notifications when fresh companies are discovered. At this point the process became fully hands-off.

Building it in stages meant the client was never asked to take a leap of faith — they saw it working at each step.

## The Result

The client went from a fragile, person-dependent routine to a dependable daily engine for new leads.

The work no longer depends on any single person's availability or attention. The coverage is far more thorough, because the system never gets tired or skips a day. The messy company-name problem is handled automatically. And because we designed it to grow, new job sites or data sources can be added later without rebuilding anything.

Most importantly, the sales team now spends its time acting on opportunities instead of hunting for them — which is exactly what they needed in the first place.

---

# AI-Powered Sales Proposal Engine 2026 Mar (FILEMAKER)

*Turning hours of manual prospect research into instant, ready-to-pitch sales recommendations, built for the Japanese market.*

---

## The Client

A sales and marketing organization serving small and medium-sized businesses across Japan. Their team helps local companies (restaurants, clinics, salons, construction firms, retailers, and more) grow through web, advertising, and local search services.

## The Challenge

Their sales team was doing everything by hand.

Before approaching any business, a salesperson had to play detective. They would open the company's website, check how fast it loaded, scroll through its Google reviews, look at its social media, guess whether its contract was up for renewal, and try to figure out what the business actually needed. Only then could they put together a pitch.

This was slow, inconsistent, and easy to get wrong. One salesperson might spot an opportunity another would miss. Good leads went cold simply because no one had time to research them properly. And because every proposal was built from scratch, the quality depended entirely on who happened to be working that day.

They had never had a system that could do this thinking for them. There was no single place that combined what they already knew about a customer with what was happening out in the real world. They needed a way to answer one simple question, instantly and reliably: *which client should we talk to next, and what should we offer them?*

## What We Built

We built an AI engine that does the research and the thinking automatically, then hands the sales team a ready-to-use proposal.

It works in two layers that come together:

**1. Understanding the customer they already have.** The engine reads the client's own records (contracts, plans, payment history, and account details) and spots the natural moments to reach out. A contract coming up for renewal. A customer using one service but missing an obvious second one. A business juggling several separate contracts that could be simplified into one. These are opportunities that were always there, just buried in data nobody had time to comb through.

**2. Reading the outside world.** For each business, the engine quietly checks dozens of public signals and turns them into plain-language insights. A few examples:

- The website loads slowly or shows "not secure" in the browser, so we can offer to fix and modernize it.
- The footer still says it was last updated in 2018, a sign the site has been neglected and is ready for a refresh.
- Social media accounts have gone quiet for months, opening the door to a management service.
- Reviews keep mentioning the same complaint (long wait times, for example), which points to a specific, useful solution.
- A competitor is outranking them on Google, or the business is hiring fast, both strong signals of the right moment to step in.
- Seasonal timing (year-end sales, Golden Week, the back-to-school rush) makes certain offers land far better.

The engine also tunes every suggestion to the local context. A pitch for a business in central Tokyo looks different from one for a shop in a regional town or a hotel in a tourist area, and the engine adjusts automatically. It even understands the language of each industry, so its suggestions sound informed rather than generic.

The result is a steady stream of timely, relevant, context-aware proposals, each one explaining not just *who* to contact but *why now* and *what to offer*.

## The Impact

What used to take a salesperson an hour of digging now happens in seconds, and it happens the same way every time, for every prospect.

- **Research becomes instant.** The team stops hunting for information and starts acting on it.
- **Quality becomes consistent.** Every proposal is built on the same thorough analysis, regardless of who's at the desk.
- **Opportunities stop slipping away.** Renewal windows, service gaps, and well-timed openings get surfaced automatically instead of being missed.
- **Pitches actually fit.** Because each suggestion reflects the client's real situation and local market, they resonate far more than a one-size-fits-all approach.

For the first time, the sales team had a system that thought like their best researcher, worked at the speed of software, and never got tired.

## The Tech, Briefly

The platform pairs the client's internal customer data with live public signals gathered from sources like Google, business listings, and review platforms. A decision layer interprets all of it and generates the final proposals, with everything optimized specifically for how small and medium businesses operate in Japan.

There was no reliable way to route approval requests, track who responded, and automatically notify dependent systems of the decision—all while keeping the conversation visible in the tools people already use every day.

The Solution

We built HAG (Human Approval Gateway)—a self-hosted service that bridges the gap between systems that need approval and the people who give it.

HAG works by listening for approval requests from any system, instantly delivering those requests as interactive Slack messages to the right people or channels, and automatically notifying the originating system once a decision is made. No more manual handoffs. No more lost approvals. No more guessing who decided what.

How It Works (In Plain Terms)

Request: Your system sends an approval request to HAG with a message, who needs to respond, and where to send the decision.
Notify: HAG immediately posts an interactive message to each recipient's Slack DM or channel.
Respond: Recipients click "Approve" or "Reject" directly in Slack.
Decide: HAG applies your approval rule (first response wins, or all must agree) and posts the decision back to your system.
The entire flow happens in seconds. Slack becomes your approval interface. Your systems stay in sync.

Key Benefits

Speed: Approval decisions go from minutes (hunting people down) to seconds (Slack notification).
Transparency: Every approval is logged, audited, and visible to stakeholders.
Reliability: No more lost messages, missed emails, or bottlenecks from unavailable approvers.
Integration: Works with any system that can make an HTTP request—no custom connectors needed.
Control: Built on open, simple rules: approve if anyone responds, or wait for everyone.
Real-World Use

HAG is used for deployment gates, high-value transaction approvals, access requests, and incident escalations—anywhere a system needs a human decision point that's fast, auditable, and can't be missed.

HAG is a self-hosted Java service, ready to deploy behind your own infrastructure, connecting your critical workflows to the approvers who matter.
